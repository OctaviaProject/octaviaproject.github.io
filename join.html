Apply now for summer 2015!

Octavia Project is a free summer program where young women from Brooklyn explore their love of science fiction and fantasy through science, tech, art, and writing.

Our program is four weeks long, running from July 7 – July 31. Workshops are Tuesdays-Fridays, 3pm-6pm, and are held at the New Lots Brooklyn Public Library.

Please note: we prioritize applications from girls living in East New York, Brownsville, Canarsie, East Flatbush, Crown Heights, Bed Stuy, and Cypress Hills.

Read more about us on DNA Info.com, Barns & Noble blog, and Huffington Post.


An Octavia Project participant will finish the program armed with:

  • An online multi-media portfolio of their work that can be used for college, job, or internship applications.
  • Strengthened skills in science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM) as well as digital-media, art, and writing.
  • Access to a network of professional women and opportunities for internships in their chosen field.

Octavia Project Participants are:

  • Young women* from Brooklyn who are going to be in grades 9, 10, 11, or 12 in the 2015-2016 school year. (*Octavia Project welcomes participants who are trans, gender non-conforming, and questioning.)
  • Available to attend 3-hour long workshops, Tuesday-Friday, from July 7-July 31.
  • Into science fiction, fantasy, fan-fiction, art, writing, science, tech, gaming—or interested in learning more about these topics!
  • Willing to try new things, boldly go where we haven't gone before, and share knowledge with others.

To apply:

Step 1: Sign up for an information session.

Step 2: Download and fill out an application. You can email your completed application to info [at] octaviaproject.org, or you can bring your filled out application to your info session. Download the application as a word doc to fill it out on the computer, or download the application as a pdf to print and fill out.

Step 3: Attend the information session you signed up for. You will learn more about the program, and then we will meet with each applicant individually to review her application. Please feel free to bring a parent/ guardian with you!
